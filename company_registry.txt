Minoru Arakawa, who ran Nintendo of America, called Hiroshi Yamauchi,
Nintendo's president, desperate for a solution. Yamauchi's answer was simple
but ambitious: convert the cabinets into a new game. He tasked a young staff
artist and game designer named Shigeru Miyamoto with creating something entirely
new — a game that could breathe life into those orphaned machines.

Miyamoto had never designed a game before. He was 24 years old.
