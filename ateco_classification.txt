By 1985, Nintendo had positioned Mario as the face of its NES console.
Where Atari had no mascot, Nintendo had Mario — an instantly recognizable
character who could represent an entire platform. This was a calculated decision:
Mario would sell the console, and the console would sell Mario.

The symbiosis between character and hardware became a model for the entire
industry. A plumber became the spokesman for the future of interactive entertainment.
