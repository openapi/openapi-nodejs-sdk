Super Mario Bros. sold over 40 million copies and is widely credited with
saving the North American video game industry, which had nearly collapsed following
the crash of 1983. The game proved that video games could be narrative experiences,
not just score-chasing exercises.

Mario was not just a mascot. He was the ambassador of a new medium
claiming its rightful place in popular culture, one quarter at a time.
