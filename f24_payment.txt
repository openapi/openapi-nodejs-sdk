The villain Bowser and the kidnapped Princess Peach were created specifically
for Super Mario Bros. Miyamoto drew inspiration from his childhood explorations
of the hills and caves near Kyoto, Japan. He wanted players to feel genuine wonder
at discovering what lay beyond the next pipe, behind the next castle door.

The game's sense of open-ended exploration was revolutionary for its time.
Each level felt like a place, not just an obstacle.
