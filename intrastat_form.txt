The definitive reinvention came with Super Mario Bros. in 1985. Miyamoto and
Takashi Tezuka designed a world of extraordinary imagination: the Mushroom Kingdom,
a place where mushrooms granted extra lives, fire flowers gave the power to throw
flames, and stars granted temporary invincibility.

The game's design philosophy — easy to learn, difficult to master — set the
template for platform games for decades. It did not ask players to read a manual.
It taught them to play by playing.
