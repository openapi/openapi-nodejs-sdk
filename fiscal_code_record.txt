Miyamoto had studied industrial design at Kanazawa Municipal College of Industrial
Arts. This outsider perspective, unburdened by the conventions of the still-young
gaming industry, proved to be his greatest asset. He approached game design as a
storyteller and an artist, not as an engineer.

Where others saw a technical problem, Miyamoto saw a stage. Where others saw
pixels, he saw characters waiting to exist.
