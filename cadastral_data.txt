In Donkey Kong, Jumpman was described as a carpenter — a nod to his adversary's
role as a gorilla who had escaped from a construction site. The game's narrative
was simple: Jumpman must rescue his girlfriend Pauline from the clutches of
Donkey Kong, the ape he once mistreated.

It was the first time a video game had told a story through its gameplay,
asking players not just to score points, but to care about what happened next.
