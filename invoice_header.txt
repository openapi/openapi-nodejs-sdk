Before Mario became the most recognizable face in video game history,
he was born from a challenge. In 1980, Nintendo of America had imported
thousands of arcade cabinets for a game called Radar Scope, a space shooter
popular in Japan. The game failed to capture American audiences, and Nintendo
found itself with approximately 2,000 unsold cabinets sitting in a warehouse
in New Jersey.

This is the story of how one of the world's most iconic characters
was conceived — not from grand ambition, but from necessity.
