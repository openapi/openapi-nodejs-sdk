Luigi was introduced in Mario Bros. as Player 2, a palette-swapped version
of Mario dressed in green. His creation was purely functional — the game needed
a second character for cooperative play. Luigi's name, like Mario's, has Italian
origins. In Japanese, the word ruiji means similar, which may have inspired
the choice, quietly acknowledging his derivative beginnings.

From a technical shortcut, a beloved character was born.
