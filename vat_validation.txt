The game Miyamoto created was Donkey Kong, released in 1981. Its protagonist
needed a name, and Miyamoto first called him Jumpman — a descriptor that captured
his one defining ability. The character's visual design was dictated entirely by
the technical limitations of the hardware: each sprite could only be a small
cluster of pixels on an 8-bit screen.

Every constraint became an opportunity.
