With Mario Bros. in 1983, Miyamoto transformed the character completely.
Jumpman the carpenter became Mario the plumber, working in the sewers beneath
New York City alongside his brother Luigi. The change was practical: the
underground pipe-and-platform setting suited a plumber far better than a carpenter.

It also allowed the brothers to emerge from and disappear into the pipes
that became a hallmark of the entire series — a portal between worlds,
a mechanic born from a profession chosen almost by accident.
