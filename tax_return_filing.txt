The character's name changed from Jumpman to Mario sometime around 1982, during
the development of Donkey Kong Junior. The story most often told is that the name
came from Mario Segale, the Italian-American landlord of Nintendo of America's
warehouse in Tukwila, Washington.

During a tense business meeting, Segale reportedly burst in demanding unpaid rent.
The room fell silent. Then someone suggested naming the character after him.
A legend was named after a confrontation over an overdue bill.
